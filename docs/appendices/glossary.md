# Glossary

## Overview

This glossary provides definitions for technical terms, acronyms, business concepts, and specialized terminology used throughout the car rental system planning and analysis documentation. Terms are organized alphabetically for easy reference.

## A

**ACRISS Codes**: Association of Car Rental Industry Systems Standards codes - a standardized classification system for rental vehicles based on type, transmission, fuel, and air conditioning.

**API (Application Programming Interface)**: A set of protocols and tools for building software applications that specify how software components should interact.

**API Gateway**: A server that acts as an intermediary between clients and backend services, handling request routing, composition, and protocol translation.

**AR (Augmented Reality)**: Technology that overlays digital information onto the real world through a device's camera, used for vehicle showrooms and wayfinding.

**Autonomous Vehicle (AV)**: A vehicle capable of sensing its environment and operating without human involvement.

**Average Order Value (AOV)**: The average dollar amount spent each time a customer completes a booking.

## B

**Backend**: The server-side of an application that handles business logic, database operations, and API endpoints.

**Biometric Authentication**: Security process that uses unique biological characteristics (fingerprint, face, iris) to verify user identity.

**Blockchain**: A distributed ledger technology that maintains a secure and decentralized record of transactions.

**BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy)**: A wireless communication technology designed for short-range communication with low power consumption, used for digital keys.

**BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later)**: A payment option that allows customers to split purchases into installments without traditional credit checks.

**Booking Lifecycle**: The complete journey of a rental booking from creation through completion, including states like pending, confirmed, active, completed, and cancelled.

## C

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: The total cost of acquiring a new customer, including marketing and sales expenses.

**CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)**: California state law regulating how businesses handle consumer personal information.

**CDN (Content Delivery Network)**: A geographically distributed network of servers that delivers web content to users based on their location.

**Circuit Breaker**: A design pattern that prevents cascading failures in distributed systems by detecting failures and stopping requests to failing services.

**Cloud-Native**: An approach to building and running applications that exploits the advantages of cloud computing delivery models.

**Contactless Operations**: Rental processes that minimize or eliminate physical contact and paperwork through digital technologies.

**Corporate Booking Tool (CBT)**: Software that enables corporate clients to manage employee travel bookings with policy enforcement and centralized billing.

**Crypto-Fiat Gateway**: A payment system that accepts cryptocurrency from customers but settles in traditional currency to eliminate volatility risk.

**CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery)**: A security vulnerability where unauthorized commands are transmitted from a user that the web application trusts.

## D

**Database per Service**: A microservices pattern where each service owns its database exclusively, ensuring loose coupling.

**Decentralized Identity (DID)**: A portable digital identity that users control, enabling reputation and verification across platforms.

**DevOps**: A set of practices that combines software development and IT operations to shorten development cycles and provide continuous delivery.

**Digital KYC (Know Your Customer)**: Automated identity verification using document scanning, facial recognition, and liveness checks.

**Digital Twin**: A virtual representation of a physical asset (vehicle) used for comparison and analysis.

**Docker**: A platform for developing, shipping, and running applications in containers.

**Dynamic Pricing**: A pricing strategy where prices adjust in real-time based on demand, supply, competitor rates, and other factors.

## E

**EARS (Easy Approach to Requirements Syntax)**: A structured method for writing clear, testable requirements using specific patterns.

**ECU (Engine Control Unit)**: An embedded system that controls one or more electrical systems in a vehicle.

**EV (Electric Vehicle)**: A vehicle that uses one or more electric motors for propulsion.

**Event-Driven Architecture**: A software architecture pattern where system components communicate through events.

**Eventual Consistency**: A consistency model where data updates propagate to all nodes eventually, but not immediately.

## F

**Fractional Ownership**: A model where multiple parties own shares of a high-value asset (vehicle) and share usage rights and revenue.

**Frontend**: The client-side of an application that users interact with directly, including web and mobile interfaces.

**Fraud Detection**: Systems and processes that identify and prevent fraudulent transactions and identity theft.

## G

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**: European Union regulation on data protection and privacy.

**Geofencing**: Creating virtual geographic boundaries that trigger actions when a device enters or exits the area.

**GDS (Global Distribution System)**: A network that enables transactions between travel service providers and travel agencies.

## H

**Horizontal Scaling**: Adding more machines or instances to distribute load, as opposed to vertical scaling (adding resources to a single machine).

**Hybrid Business Model**: A platform that supports multiple business models simultaneously (B2C, P2P, subscription, corporate).

## I

**Immutable**: Data that cannot be changed after creation, a key property of blockchain records.

**IoT (Internet of Things)**: Network of physical devices embedded with sensors and software that connect and exchange data.

**ISO (International Organization for Standardization)**: An international standard-setting body that develops and publishes standards.

## J

**JWT (JSON Web Token)**: A compact, URL-safe means of representing claims to be transferred between two parties, commonly used for authentication.

## K

**Kubernetes (K8s)**: An open-source container orchestration platform for automating deployment, scaling, and management of containerized applications.

**KYC (Know Your Customer)**: The process of verifying the identity of clients to prevent fraud and money laundering.

## L

**Liveness Check**: A biometric security measure that verifies a person is physically present (not a photo or video) during authentication.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**: The predicted net profit attributed to the entire future relationship with a customer.

## M

**Microservices**: An architectural style that structures an application as a collection of loosely coupled, independently deployable services.

**Mini-Program**: A lightweight application that runs within a super-app (like WeChat) without requiring separate installation.

**Monolith**: An architectural style where all application components are tightly integrated into a single codebase and deployment unit.

**MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport)**: A lightweight messaging protocol for IoT devices with limited bandwidth.

**Multi-Tenancy**: A software architecture where a single instance serves multiple customers (tenants) with data isolation.

**MVC (Model-View-Controller)**: A software design pattern that separates application logic into three interconnected components.

## N

**NFC (Near Field Communication)**: A short-range wireless technology that enables communication between devices in close proximity.

**NFT (Non-Fungible Token)**: A unique digital asset stored on a blockchain, used for digital collectibles and ownership proof.

**NLP (Natural Language Processing)**: AI technology that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

**NoSQL**: A category of database management systems that don't use traditional relational database structures.

## O

**OAuth**: An open standard for access delegation commonly used for token-based authentication.

**OCR (Optical Character Recognition)**: Technology that converts images of text into machine-readable text data.

**ODM (Object Document Mapper)**: A library that maps between objects in code and documents in a database (like Mongoose for MongoDB).

**Offline Mode**: Application functionality that works without internet connectivity by caching data locally.

**ORM (Object-Relational Mapping)**: A technique that converts data between incompatible type systems in object-oriented programming and relational databases.

**Overbooking**: Accepting more bookings than available inventory based on predicted no-shows and cancellations.

## P

**P2P (Peer-to-Peer)**: A business model where individuals rent vehicles directly to other individuals through a platform.

**PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)**: Security standards for organizations that handle credit card information.

**Persona**: A fictional character representing a user type with specific goals, behaviors, and pain points.

**Polyglot Persistence**: Using different database technologies for different services based on their specific needs.

**Predictive Maintenance**: Using data analysis and machine learning to predict when vehicle maintenance is needed before failures occur.

**PWA (Progressive Web App)**: A web application that uses modern web capabilities to deliver an app-like experience.

## R

**RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)**: An access control method that assigns permissions to users based on their roles.

**Redis**: An in-memory data structure store used as a database, cache, and message broker.

**REST (Representational State Transfer)**: An architectural style for designing networked applications using HTTP requests.

**RevPAU (Revenue Per Available Unit)**: A metric measuring revenue generated per available vehicle or ancillary item.

**RPC (Remote Procedure Call)**: A protocol that allows a program to execute a procedure on another computer as if it were local.

## S

**SaaS (Software as a Service)**: A software distribution model where applications are hosted by a provider and accessed over the internet.

**Saga Pattern**: A design pattern for managing distributed transactions across microservices through a sequence of local transactions.

**Serverless**: A cloud computing model where the cloud provider manages server infrastructure and automatically allocates resources.

**Service Discovery**: The automatic detection of services in a network, essential for microservices architectures.

**Service Mesh**: An infrastructure layer that handles service-to-service communication in microservices architectures.

**Smart Contract**: Self-executing contracts with terms directly written into code on a blockchain.

**SOC (State of Charge)**: The level of charge of an electric vehicle battery relative to its capacity.

**Social Login**: Authentication using credentials from social media platforms (Google, Facebook, Apple).

**SQL (Structured Query Language)**: A standard language for managing and manipulating relational databases.

**Subscription Model**: A business model where customers pay recurring fees for ongoing access to vehicles.

**Super-App**: A mobile application that provides multiple services within a single platform (like WeChat or Grab).

**Synthetic Identity Fraud**: Fraud using fabricated identities created by combining real and fake information.

## T

**Telematics**: Technology that combines telecommunications and informatics to transmit data about vehicle location, status, and diagnostics.

**Teleoperation**: Remote control of a vehicle by a human operator, often used as a bridge to full autonomy.

**TLS (Transport Layer Security)**: A cryptographic protocol that provides secure communication over a computer network.

**Tokenization**: The process of replacing sensitive data with unique identification symbols (tokens) that retain essential information without compromising security.

**2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)**: A security process requiring two different authentication factors to verify identity.

## U

**UX (User Experience)**: The overall experience a person has when interacting with a product or service.

**UI (User Interface)**: The visual elements and controls through which users interact with a system.

## V

**V2G (Vehicle-to-Grid)**: Technology that enables electric vehicles to send power back to the electrical grid.

**Vertical Scaling**: Adding more resources (CPU, RAM) to a single machine, as opposed to horizontal scaling.

**VIN (Vehicle Identification Number)**: A unique code assigned to every motor vehicle for identification.

**VR (Virtual Reality)**: Computer-generated simulation of a three-dimensional environment that users can interact with.

## W

**WAV (Wheelchair Accessible Vehicle)**: A vehicle modified to accommodate wheelchair users.

**WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)**: International standards for making web content accessible to people with disabilities.

**Webhook**: A method for applications to provide real-time information to other applications by sending HTTP callbacks.

## X

**XSS (Cross-Site Scripting)**: A security vulnerability where attackers inject malicious scripts into web pages viewed by other users.
